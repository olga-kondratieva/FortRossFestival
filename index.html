Location and park info

Fort Ross is a California State Historic Park showcasing a historic Russian-era fort compound that has been designated National Historic Landmark status. Located eleven miles north of Jenner on California Highway One, one of the most scenic coastal routes in the world Fort Ross is surrounded by sandy beaches, panoramic coves, and redwood forests, with breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.

History of Fort Ross

Fort Ross is the American name for the Russian fortress and a settlement in California that existed from 1812 to 1841.

This settlement has been organized through the initiative of the Company. Its purpose is to establish a settlement there or in some other place not occupied by Europeans, and to introduce agriculture there by planting hemp, flax and all manner of garden produce; they also wish to introduce livestock breeding in the outlying areas, both horses and cattle, hoping that the favorable climate, which is almost identical to the rest of California, and the friendly reception on the part of the indigenous people, will assist in its success.

From an 1813 report to Emperor Alexander from the Russian American Company Council, concerning trade with California and the establishment of Fort Ross

Fort Ross was founded by a Russian-American company and was the southernmost Russian colony in North America. Fort Ross was created as an agricultural settlement designed to supply Russian Alaska with food. In the early years, the company also paid attention to the development of fur trade in Ross, but agriculture and small industry became the basis of its economy.

In 1841, the company sold its property to large landowner John Sutter. Currently, it is a part of California State Historic Park, a California Historical Landmark and a National Historic Landmark.
